DATA ANALYSIS
1. Data Collection & Cleaning
• I downloaded the dataset from an online source.
• Removed missing values using mean imputation.
• Verified datatypes and checked for outliers.
• Separated the dataset into features (X) and label (y).
The purpose of data cleaning was to make the dataset suitable for ML algorithms.

2. Data Analysis
I performed basic analysis to understand how each feature affects the default risk.
Examples you can tell:
• Higher credit utilization increases default chances
• More late payments increase risk
• Lower income and higher debt ratio often lead to defaults
This shows understanding.

3. Splitting the Data
I divided the data into 80% training and 20% testing to evaluate model performance.

4. Algorithms Used
As per the assignment guidelines, I applied three algorithms:

A. Decision Tree Classifier
• Simple rule-based model
• Learns conditions like “If utilization > 0.89 and income < X, then default”
• Accuracy: ~ 89.73%
Decision Tree helped me understand the decision rules behind credit risk.

B. Random Forest Classifier
• An ensemble of many decision trees
• Reduces overfitting
• Gave the best accuracy of around 93.67%
Random Forest performed best because it combines multiple trees to give stable predictions.

C. Logistic Regression
• Suitable for binary classification
• Works well after feature scaling
• Accuracy: ~ 93.50%
Logistic regression gave good results because the dataset is numeric and linear relationships
exist.

5. Evaluation Metrics
For each model, I calculated:
• Accuracy Score
• Confusion Matrix
The confusion matrix helps me understand how many defaults and non-defaults were
correctly predicted.

Conclusion
Among all three models, Random Forest achieved the highest accuracy and provided the most
reliable results. It is recommended for credit risk prediction due to its robustness and ability to
handle complex data.
